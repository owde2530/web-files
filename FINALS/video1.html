Julius Caesar High School (writer/director, 2016)

(full video link - videos above are previews only)

For my final high shcool video production class, students were tasked with making a short film that imitates a famous film director's style. I chose Wes Anderson, as he's a bit of a favorite of mine, and I've been watching his movies for a while. In particular, I took a lot of inspiration from Rushmore. The story follows a high school student bullied and ostracized by most of the other kids in his class. He gets fed up, and, in a glorious display of rebellion, he decides to humiliate his prime bully in the middle of a crowded passing period. Definitely Wes Anderson, but with a surprisingly dark side to it as well, I'd definitely say this is one of my favorite pieces I've made so far.
