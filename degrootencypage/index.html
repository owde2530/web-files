Defintion

Cowboy (kaù-boì)
noun: A person commonly tasked with shepparding cattle.
A common occupation in the mid to late nineteenth century. Has since become a pop culture icon, both
in and outside of America.

History

More detailed information can be found here.

In the age of Westward expansion in the United States, in the mid 19th century to early 20th century,
cowboys were a common occupation, depiste their present illustriousness. Cowboys were workers who typically
herded cattle over long distances, on a seasonsal basis. At the time, this was considered a respectable and
commonplace job. However, with time, the job took on a more nebulous role of that of the pioneer, the bandit,
all morphed into this general conception of "cowboy."

Legacy

Cowboys have since become an icon of sort in American culture. At home and abroad, the term cowboy
can mean to say cavalier or baudy in a uniquely American way. The British sense of the word cowboy, in
in fact, means just this.

Famous Depictions in Popular Culture

  • John Wayne commonly played cowboys in many famous Spaghetti Western Movies.
  • The Japanese Animated TV show, Cowboy Bebop, famously riffed on the general
    conception of a cowboy
  • Back to the Future Pt. 3, wherein the classic character Marty McFly travels
    back in time to the Wild West, taking the role of a cowboy.
